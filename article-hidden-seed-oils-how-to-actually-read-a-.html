Hidden Seed Oils: How to Actually Read a Food Label

Share

I still get surprised when I show someone a label on something they eat every day and they have no idea there is vegetable oil in it. Crackers, marinades, baby food, protein bars, stuff that sounds completely fine. This quick guide is for anyone who wants to clean up their trolley without having to go back to university to do it.

The Names They Hide Behind

Seed oils, think canola, sunflower, safflower, soybean, cottonseed, corn oil and rice bran oil, are in probably half the packaged food in your supermarket. They rarely get called out clearly. Instead you will see terms like "vegetable oil", "vegetable fat", or "blended oil" which is basically a cocktail of the cheapest oils the manufacturer could source that week. These oils are high in omega-6 polyunsaturated fats, the kind that oxidise easily under heat, like butter left on a hot pan that goes dark and acrid, except this is happening inside a factory at extreme temperatures before the food even hits the shelf. In our house we call them the sneaky ones because they hide in foods that sound wholesome.

They rarely call it seed oil on the label. They call it vegetable oil and hope you keep walking.

The Simple Trolley Guide

My honest default is to avoid boxes and barcodes altogether and build meals from fruit, vegetables, meat, eggs and raw whole foods. But real life is not always that clean, and I get that. So here is the practical version. Pick up the packet and go straight to the ingredients list, not the nutrition panel. Scan for any of these words: canola, sunflower, safflower, soybean, corn, cottonseed, rice bran, or anything that just says vegetable oil or vegetable fat. If you see them in the first four or five ingredients, put the packet back. Manufacturers list ingredients by weight, so if the oil is near the top it is in there in a meaningful amount, not just a trace. A handful of genuinely good options do exist in packaging, things made with olive oil, coconut oil, butter or animal fats, and they are worth tracking down once you know what you are looking for.

One thing to do today: grab three packaged items from your pantry right now, flip them over and scan the ingredients list for vegetable oil or any of the names above. Just see what is in there. That is it. No overhaul needed today, just awareness.
